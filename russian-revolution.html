Russian Revolution (1917)

The Russian Revolution of 1917, led by the Bolsheviks and Vladimir Lenin, reshaped Russian and global history. Amid World War I and widespread discontent, the Bolsheviks overthrew the Russian Provisional Government, establishing a socialist state. The revolution inspired leftist movements worldwide and heightened fears of communism among conservatives. This ideological divide set the stage for the 20th century's struggles. The aftermath brought profound changes to Russia. The Soviet Union emerged as the first socialist state, initiating radical social and economic shifts. Globally, the revolution fueled anti-communist sentiments and heightened Cold War tensions. Its impact endured, influencing the global political landscape and shaping socialist movements for decades.
