Soviet Collectivization and Purges (1930s)

Stalin's policies in the Soviet Union during the 1930s involved forced collectivization and purges. Collectivization aimed to consolidate peasant farms into collective farms, but resulted in a decline in agricultural output and the devastating Holodomor famine. The purges targeted perceived political threats, leading to show trials, executions, and a climate of fear and suspicion. These actions showcased the brutality of totalitarian regimes and solidified Stalin's authoritarian rule.
