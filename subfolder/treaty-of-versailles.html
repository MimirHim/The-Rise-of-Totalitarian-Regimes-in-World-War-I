Treaty of Versailles (1919)

The Treaty of Versailles, which was ratified in 1919 as a peace accord to bring World War I to a close and restructure the global order, imposed stringent conditions on Germany. Holding Germany primarily accountable for the war, the treaty demanded substantial territorial and economic concessions, including punitive reparations, territorial forfeitures, and disarmament provisions designed to weaken Germany both militarily and economically. Nevertheless, the severity of the treaty contributed to instabilities in both social and economic realms within Germany, establishing a conducive environment for the emergence of totalitarian regimes.
